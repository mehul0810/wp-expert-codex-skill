# Enterprise Design Judgment

Use this as a shared design-sense overlay for premium/enterprise WordPress product, site, dashboard, onboarding, docs, and marketing work. This is a judgment standard, not a visual-copying instruction.

Use it when the surface has buyer, stakeholder, workflow, governance, compliance, operational-risk, or trust weight. For example: admin UI, approvals, AI/workflow products, onboarding, settings, docs IA, enterprise landing pages, case-study pages, dashboards, and release-proof screens.

## Core Principle

Enterprise design is not just polished UI. It must make accountability, control, proof, and failure behavior legible.

For every meaningful surface, ask:

- Who is responsible for this action or outcome?
- What operational risk is reduced or governed?
- What workflow, approval, policy, or audit expectation exists?
- What proof makes the claim credible?
- What happens when the workflow fails, is blocked, or needs review?

If the UI or page cannot answer those questions, it is probably still too generic.

## Preferred Enterprise Signals

Prefer:

- Real product screenshots, real workflow states, and real controlled UI over abstract decorative mockups.
- Buyer-, role-, industry-, or task-based IA over generic feature buckets.
- Quantified trust signals, customer/use-case proof, case-study evidence, and operational outcomes.
- Calm, restrained, confident visual language over novelty-first styling.
- Clear language around governance, approvals, permissions, auditability, reliability, integrations, and failure handling.
- Evidence that a system works under pressure, at scale, or with review/compliance constraints.

Avoid:

- Generic SaaS cards, decorative gradients/orbs, or trend-driven visual filler that adds no proof.
- Vague AI claims without policy, approvals, context, or reliability framing.
- Feature lists with no operational consequence, proof, or user-outcome connection.
- Screens that look polished but hide ownership, failure paths, or review controls.

## IA And Messaging Rules

- Organize enterprise pages and product surfaces around accountable roles, jobs, use cases, or risk areas.
- Lead with what the system governs, accelerates, or makes safer, not with implementation trivia.
- Prefer proof-led hierarchy: claim, evidence, workflow, control, outcome.
- Use customer stories, metrics, screenshots, and concrete workflow examples to support claims.
- Show how the product fits existing editorial, engineering, compliance, or support systems.
- When AI is involved, describe governed workflow value before model novelty.

## AI And Workflow Products

For AI, automation, and workflow-heavy products:

- Frame value around governed workflows, approvals, auditability, policy, reliability, and integration into existing systems.
- Show where human review exists, what permissions apply, what gets logged, and how failure or retry is handled.
- Prefer screenshots or state diagrams that show controls, approvals, and outcomes instead of generic AI chat visuals.
- Avoid implying autonomy without guardrails, review boundaries, or recovery behavior.

## UX And UI Review Lens

For enterprise/premium review, check:

- Is the primary accountable user obvious?
- Does the IA match buyer/stakeholder mental models?
- Are proof and trust signals specific, quantified, and relevant?
- Are approvals, permissions, audit trails, or workflow checkpoints visible where they matter?
- Does the design show what happens on failure, delay, or blocked action?
- Does the visual language feel controlled and trustworthy instead of trendy or noisy?
- Are screenshots, case-study evidence, metrics, docs links, or operational artifacts stronger than decorative filler?
